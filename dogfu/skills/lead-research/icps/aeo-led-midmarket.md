# ICP 2 (Exploratory) — AEO-Led Mid-Market B2B (10–50M ARR)

> **Exploratory, not the primary motion.** ICP 1 (`founder-led-saas-scaleup`) is the
> committed bullseye and the default. This is a deliberate higher-end experiment: an
> established mid-market B2B company, led into with **AEO** rather than classic SEO.
> Qualify against this only when the caller names it, and flag every fit as part of the
> experimental upmarket track, not the proven motion.

## Who we are

We're an AI SEO/AEO platform. Up-market we lead with **AEO (answer-engine
optimization)**: we map which sources the LLMs actually pull a category's answers from and
act on it faster and cheaper than an SEO-era agency or in-house team can. Classic
SEO/organic work is the **expansion**, sold once we're inside and trusted.

## Who we target (must-haves)

- **Segment:** an established **B2B** company (SaaS-leaning, but vertical open — same soft
  treatment as ICP 1; this whole track is itself an experiment).
- **Stage / size:** roughly **$10M–$50M ARR.** Established and resourced, with a real
  marketing/growth org and budget.
- **Buyer:** **Head of Growth / VP Marketing / CMO** — a professional buyer with budget
  authority. Expect a multi-threaded, longer sales motion (committee, procurement).
- **AEO opening (the wedge):** decent classic SEO but **weak or absent AI-answer
  visibility** — a real, current AEO gap to lead with. This gap is what makes the
  higher-end buyer reachable without case studies: nobody has AEO case studies yet, so the
  usual reference-hungry, logo-wall expectation largely evaporates *in AEO*. We sell on
  thesis and on being visibly ahead.

## Disqualifiers

- Builds / sells / markets SEO/AEO capability to others (competitor — the skill's gate).
- No marketing/growth org or budget — that's the lean-team motion (ICP 1), not this one.
- **No discernible AEO gap and no classic-SEO surface to expand into** — nothing to lead
  with and nothing to grow into.
- Demands we **replace their stack / run on their infrastructure** rather than adopt the
  product as the substrate.

## Decision-maker

Head of Growth / VP Marketing / CMO. Expect more than one stakeholder — map the buying
committee, not a single signer.

## Notes for scoring

- This buyer is the one ICP 1 marks "weak / avoid (enterprise, procurement)." Here that is
  **intentional**: the AEO-led entry is the whole reason an otherwise reference-hungry buyer
  is reachable. Score the **AEO gap** heavily — a strong classic-SEO company that is
  invisible in AI answers is the bullseye for *this* ICP.
- **Strong:** 10–50M B2B with a clear AEO gap and an addressable classic-SEO surface to
  expand into. **Partial:** right size/buyer but only a soft AEO gap, or notably larger/
  smaller. **Weak:** no AEO gap and no expansion surface, or a hard stack-replacement
  demand.
- Size is a soft guide and not directly measurable (`dogfu` has no revenue signal — infer
  from headcount/funding). Flag size caveats rather than auto-disqualifying.
- **Exploratory status:** treat fits as candidates to learn from, and say plainly in the
  CRM note that this came through the experimental upmarket track.
