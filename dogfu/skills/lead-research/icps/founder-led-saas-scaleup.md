# ICP 1 — Founder-Led, SEO-Active B2B SaaS/Marketplace Scaleups

Fast-growing B2B / B2B2C SaaS or marketplace companies where a **founder (or a
founder-delegated growth/marketing lead) signs**, there is already a **1–3 person in-house
marketing/SEO function** doing real SEO work, and the buyer personally understands SEO/AEO
well enough to operate a platform like ours with their own team.

This is the bullseye — the shape of the customer we already win. The job is to find more of
exactly this.

## Who we are

We're an AI SEO/AEO platform that replaces hiring an SEO agency or building a large
in-house SEO team. We redirect the SEO / content / agency budget a company **already
spends**; we don't ask for net-new budget. (This also drives the skill's
competitive-conflict gate: companies that *build or sell* SEO/AEO capability to others are
competitors, not customers.)

## Who we target (must-haves)

- **Segment:** B2B / B2B2C **SaaS or marketplace.** Vertical is a *cluster*, not a hard
  gate — see "Vertical is deliberately open" below.
- **Stage / size:** a fast-growing scaleup, roughly **$20k–$150k+ MRR and climbing**
  (~$250k–$1.8M+ ARR). A **soft guide**, not a cutoff — and not directly measurable (see
  scoring notes).
- **In-house execution capacity:** a **1–3 person marketing/SEO team**, or a hands-on
  operator-founder doing the SEO themselves. There must be *someone in-house who can carry
  execution* — this is what makes the account work.
- **Active SEO:** already doing real SEO / content work — not starting from zero. They
  already spend on SEO / content / an agency, so adopting our platform is a **reallocation**, not
  a new budget line.
- **Buyer:** a **founder, or a founder-delegated growth/marketing lead** — someone who can
  give a fast, single-threaded yes. Thesis-driven, buys on conviction, typically active on
  LinkedIn.
- **Buyer literacy (strong positive):** the buyer personally understands SEO/AEO well
  enough to operate a platform. AEO / AI-search awareness (or anxiety), and AI already in
  the marketing workflow, are strong fit signals — strong positives, not hard gates.

## Disqualifiers

(The competitor exclusion is enforced by the skill's competitive-conflict gate; these are
the practical "no"s on top of it.)

- **No in-house marketing function** — an owner-run SMB / local-services business with no
  marketing team and no operator-founder to carry execution. We'd have to run everything
  ourselves; wrong economics. Take inbound, don't hunt.
- **Not actually doing SEO yet** — no existing content/SEO motion to build on.
- A buyer who wants a **done-for-you agency** and won't operate a platform with their team.
- **Enterprise / procurement-driven** buying — RFPs, committees, multi-threaded sign-off,
  case-study/reference expectations we can't yet meet. Right buyer eventually, wrong motion
  now. (For the deliberate upmarket experiment, see ICP 2 — `aeo-led-midmarket`.)

## Decision-maker

Founder first; otherwise the founder-delegated **Head of Growth / Marketing / SEO**. At
this size the two often collapse into one person. Capture both when both plausibly own the
SEO/AEO decision.

## Notes for scoring (primary / secondary / avoid → strong / partial / weak)

Map the verdict tiers onto the customer shapes we've graded:

- **Strong (bullseye):** founder-led, fast-growing B2B SaaS/marketplace with a 1–3 person
  marketing/SEO team, already doing real SEO, literate / AEO-aware buyer. Hits the
  must-haves.
- **Partial (secondary):** a **Head of Marketing / Growth lead at a mid-market company**
  with a real team and budget — same shape, slower, more reference-sensitive. Also: a
  founder-led fit that sits **outside the revenue band** or in an **adjacent vertical** but
  otherwise matches — flag the caveat, don't down-rank to weak.
- **Weak / avoid:** **no-team SMB / local services** (no execution capacity, wrong
  economics); and **CMO/VP enterprise** with procurement/RFP buying and a case-study
  expectation. Right buyers someday, wrong fit for this motion now.

**Size is a soft guide, not a measurable gate.** `dogfu` exposes no MRR/revenue signal —
infer scale loosely from headcount and funding. Don't disqualify a behaviorally strong
lead for being above or below the band; flag the size caveat and let the human decide.

### Vertical is deliberately open

This persona *clusters* in B2B SaaS / marketplaces (also where AI-search anxiety runs
hottest), but the vertical is **not yet committed** — we're still validating it and will
deliberately try close-adjacent profiles. So **do not auto-disqualify on vertical alone**:
a company with the right *buyer shape and SEO behavior* (founder-led, in-house SEO team,
active SEO, literate buyer) in a *different* B2B vertical is a **partial fit worth
surfacing**, not a "no." Treat segment like the revenue band — a soft cluster signal,
flagged, not a hard gate.
